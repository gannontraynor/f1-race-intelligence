# F1 Race Intelligence Platform

## Vision

Modern Formula 1 fans have access to an enormous amount of race data, but very little of it is presented in a way that helps answer meaningful questions.

This project aims to bridge that gap by transforming publicly available timing, telemetry, and race data into an interactive intelligence platform.

Rather than acting as another statistics website, the platform focuses on helping users understand **why** races unfolded the way they did.

Users should be able to investigate:

- Why one driver was faster than another
- How tire strategy influenced the race
- Where time was gained or lost
- The effectiveness of pit stop timing
- Race-defining moments
- Team strategy decisions
- Driver consistency
- Pace evolution throughout a race

Ultimately, the goal is to create a platform that combines engineering, analytics, visualization, simulation, and AI into a single experience that helps users explore Formula 1 at a deeper level.

---

## Product Principles

The platform should always prioritize:

- Engineering over hype
- Explainable analytics over black-box conclusions
- Interactive exploration over static reports
- Deterministic calculations before AI interpretation
- Fast user experience
- Beautiful visualization
- Reproducible analysis

AI should explain results—not invent them.

---

## Long-Term Vision

The platform should eventually evolve into an engineering-grade motorsport analytics application capable of:

- Race review
- Driver comparison
- Strategy analysis
- Telemetry exploration
- Predictive simulation
- Natural language querying
- AI-assisted race explanation
- Multi-series support (IndyCar, WEC, IMSA, Formula E)
